Milk Script

Milk Script was discovered by Alfredo Graziani and Alejandro Paul in a 1923 Speedball lettering manual. The typeface is defined by dramatic majuscules and flowing minuscules. It was originally developed for hand-lettered signage, which was characteristic of 1920s and 1930s America. Its digital form now features amongst other typefaces of Sudtipos- a collective Argentinian type foundry- which produces fonts for branding, packaging, and editorial.
