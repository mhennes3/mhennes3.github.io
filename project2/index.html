Decorate for Psychological Comfort

Explore this site, and learn how to optimize the physical spaces in your home through the selection and/or orientation of objects for one's particular wellbeing.

Living Room

A living room is a space where users may both entertain guests, and rest/rejuvenate alone. As such, the elements of this room should be considered for how they may maximize the needs and wellbeing of both parties.

Kitchen

A kitchen is a space for food preparation. Unlike the living room, a kitchen is more primarily communal. Its elements should be considered in this context, while including consideration of the owner's particular wellbeing.

Bedroom

A bedroom is a space of peace and privacy. In contrast to the kitchen, a bedroom is primarily a personal domain. Therefore, its elements may unselfishly cater to the unique needs and wellbeing of the space's owner.
